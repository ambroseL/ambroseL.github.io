About Me

Lidong is my nature


Lidong is a solar term meaning “winter begins” in Chinese. Born in a season of deep discovery and imaginative creation, my nature is to think profoundly and bring creativity to life.



HOW DO I GET INTO DESIGN

Doing the things right v.s. doing the right things


A current graduate student in Human-Computer Interaction/design and a previous undergrad in Software Engineering, I realize technologies without human-centered thinking end up in unintended consequences, and design without technical support is hard to reach a wide audience. So I choose to adopt both to strive for simple but effective answers in problem-solving.

MY DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

I discover the details, connect the dots and draw a new picture holistically


For me, the nature of design lies in three fundamental questions: How did this happen? What is the problem? How to solve it? While the first two outline the problematic reality, the last one portrays the paths to the desired future. And design is the bridge. Accordingly, the way how we answer these questions in design reflects our design philosophy.

In my education, I’ve been trained for two mindsets. One is to understand and tackle problems by thorough analysis and rigorous examination. The other is to think outside the box, keep asking why and what if during problem-solving. Together, I discover the details, connect the dots, and draw a new picture holistically during a design process.
