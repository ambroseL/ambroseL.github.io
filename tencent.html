TENCENT MARKET RESEARCH CLOUD, UX DESIGN

Template-based Data Import

Facilitate data import for Tencent market researchers with spreadsheet templates

Duration

8 weeks

Role

UX Design Intern

Tools

Figma, Principle

Working as an individual designer in a cross-functional team, my job included:

  • Researched and designed the new data import from scratch
  • Explored product hypotheses by rapid prototyping
  • Refined the visual and interactive details through constant evaluations

Problem

Combining market research data is time-intensive and hard to control

Market researchers at Tencent are responsible for discovering investment opportunities from tons of market data. Currently, they’re challenged by two critical issues when getting their data together:

  • It is time-consuming to coordinate data formats across teams
  • It is hard to control data organizations in database

Solution

Facilitate data import with spreadsheet templates

In the form of sheet templates, the new data import of Tencent market research cloud saves users’ time and effort as:

  • Facilitating data coordination by consistent standards
  • Simplifying data configurations by friendly UI

01. Bring in external data by standards

To integrate data, users can first download a spreadsheet template generated by the system and copy their data to the file. Then they can upload it and preview the content. After ensuring everything is correct, the data will be stored in the market research cloud.

02. Organize data in the spreadsheet interface

To organize data collections, administrators can configure them as spreadsheets whose columns are filterable, sortable, and hidable. The result will also be used as an upload template for normal users.

Problem 1: Inefficient coordination of data formats across teams

Reporting collected data to their managers is part of Tencent market researchers’ daily work. Due to the varied data sources and formats, both sides spend a lot of time coordinating their spreadsheets manually.

FINAL CONCEPT

Introduce template-based data import

Noticing the potential value of spreadsheet templates, I took one step further by introducing it to data import as the MVP. With this new feature, market research teams can gain more data control and save time on data coordination:

  • For admins, they can easily set up data organizations and provide them as spreadsheet templates
  • For normal users, they can follow the templates to upload data on their own

DESIGN FOCUS TWO

Promote efficient coordination of varied data formats

Since data coordination usually cannot be finished within minutes, I broke it down into several steps so that users can easily know what to do at each stage:



To help the participants evaluate the complete workflow, I built a series of high-fi prototypes for my usability testing:

I learned about two major concerns:

  • Users felt it was too lengthy to finish the process across three pages
  • Users had to check back with the web instructions frequently while working on the template

Noticing the wizard didn't fit well into the user mental model, and data import was actually not so complicated, I decided to:

  • Put all the steps under one single page
  • Simplify web instructions and move more details to the template

The all-in-one version was proved to reach a balance between work efficiency and space usage in the later testing. While providing detailed instructions for first-time learners, it allowed experienced users to skip unnecessary information in the workflow.
